submits, upon being served with the garnishee order , her client acted in

sub-section (3) in section 45-G.  
3.   It is true that sub-section (2) in section 45-G do es not 
mention fixed deposit. It is not an amount exempt f rom attachment in 
execution of decree of a civil Court under section 60 of the Code of 
Civil Procedure, 1908. However, clause (iv) under sub-section 45-G (3) 
provides that in complying with a garnishee order, it shall not be 
necessary for, inter alia, any deposit receipt to b e produced to the 
person served (in this case the bank) for purpose o f any entry, 
endorsement or like being made before payment is ma de, 
notwithstanding, any rule, practice or requirement to the contrary. This 
mandate obviated the necessity of the bank from obt aining instructions 
to encash prematurely the fixed deposits, for payme nt in compliance of 
the garnishee order. Hence, there was no reference to petitioner in the 
compliance made by the bank.  
4.   For reasons aforesaid, no interference is warrante d  
5.   The writ petition is dismissed.
