Sub:- Advisory to prevent fake Insurance claims. 
  Instances have come to notice that false vehicle 
accident cases are registered in different police stations 
of the State to facilitate fake Insurance claim by the 
fraudulent claimants, in connivance with local police, 
Govt. doctors and other middlemen/ brokers. The 
Hon'ble High Court while hearing a matter of 
Insurance fraud in CRLMP No. 1209 of 2019 filed by 
ICICI Lombard General Insurance Company Limited -
Vrs- State of Odisha and others has directed the State 
to intimate about the remedial measures taken to

Page 21 of 29 
 
prevent filing of false accident cases and Insurance 
claims. 
  In order to prevent such fake accident cases and 
consequential insurance claims for claiming Insurance 
in illegal and arbitrary manner, the following 
instructions are imparted for strict adherence to curb 
menace of such issue. 
1. In all accident cases, I.Os shall visit spot 
immediately. Photography / Videography of scene 
of crime and Involved vehicles shall be taken. FIRS 
of MV accidents being reported after delay of 5-7 
days should be looked into suspiciously and 
meticulously investigated, supervised by a G.O. to 
ascertain merit of such type of cases. 
2. All accident cases should be reviewed by a police 
officer of DSP rank before submission of FF. 
3. The doctors should be sensitized to record brief of the 
accident including the category of vehicle causing 
the accident. 
4. The I.O. while verifying the Insurance details of the 
offending vehicle will Immediately intimate the 
concerned Insurance Company with regard to 
details of Injured and dead persons. 
5. The I.O. will take immediate steps for cancellation of 
DL of the driver of the offending vehicle.

46. In Vineet Narain v. Union of India 6, the Supreme Court 
had held that: 
"8. The sum and substance of these orders is that 
the CBI and other governmental agencies had not 
carried out their public duty to investigate the 
offences disclosed; that none stands above the law 
so that an alleged offence by him is not required to 
be investigated; that we would monitor the 
investigations, in the sense that we would do what 
we permissibly could to see that the investigations 
progressed while yet ensuring that we did not 
direct or channel those investigations or in any 
other manner prejudice the right of those who 
might be accused to a full and fair trial. We made 
it clear that the task of the monitoring court 
would end the moment a charge-sheet was filed in 
respect of a particular investigation and that the 
ordinary processes of the law would then take 
over. Having regard to the direction in which the 
investigations were leading, we found it necessary 
to direct the CBI not to report the progress of the 
investigations to the person occupying the highest 
office in the political executive; this was done to 
eliminate any impression of bias or lack of fairness 
or objectivity and to maintain the credibility of the 
investigations.”
